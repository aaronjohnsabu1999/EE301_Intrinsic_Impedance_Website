Variation of Intrinsic Impedance: A Comparison of Diamond and Graphite
  - Contributed by Aaron John Sabu



1. Carbon is the sixth element of the periodic table. It can take several forms such as graphite, diamond, C-60 etc.
2. Each form, though similar in element, are different in the structural composition. This leads to variation in physical properties, such as conductivity $\sigma$, relative permeability $\mu_r$ and relative permittivity \( \epsilon_r \).
3. The intrinsic impedance of a material is given as: \[ \sqrt\frac{j\omega\mu}{\sigma+j\omega\epsilon}. \] Navigate through the buttons to learn how the intrinsic impedance varies between diamond, graphite and vacuum.
